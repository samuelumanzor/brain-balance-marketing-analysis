# Brain Balance — Lead Conversion & Marketing Analytics

**The Business Question:** Across 3 San Diego-area Brain Balance centers, which marketing channels are actually driving enrollments — and where is the funnel breaking down?

This project analyzes 2,662 inquiries across 8 lead sources to identify conversion bottlenecks, channel ROI, and revenue trends.

---

## Live Dashboard

Open `brain_balance_dashboard.html` in any browser to explore the interactive dashboard with 4 tabbed sections:

- **Overview** — KPI summary and full funnel visualization (2,662 → 108 enrollments)
- **Sources** — Channel-by-channel conversion rates, bubble chart, and full breakdown table
- **Revenue** — Monthly trend (Nov 2025 – Apr 2026), month-over-month momentum
- **Centers** — Revenue share by location and staff performance comparison

---

## Key Findings

**1. The funnel breaks at booking, not closing**
2,662 inquiries → 350 appointments set → 108 enrolled. Once families come in for an assessment, 30% enroll. The real bottleneck is getting them through the door — only 13% of inquiries book an appointment.

**2. Referrals are the highest-ROI channel by far**
Only 46 referral inquiries but 21.7% enroll — 5× the average. A structured referral program would directly multiply the best-converting lead type at near-zero cost.

**3. Events generate volume, not revenue**
Events are the 2nd largest source (517 inquiries) but only 0.4% enroll — 2 families total. That's ~515 wasted follow-up calls. Budget and staff time spent on events likely has negative ROI.

**4. Phone inquiries convert 2.5× better than digital**
Phone In converts at 10.5% vs. digital sources averaging 4.2%. High-intent signal — these leads should get immediate priority follow-up.

**5. Revenue grew +81% in 6 months**
From $59K (Nov 2025) to $107K (Apr 2026). San Diego drives 53% of total revenue ($261K of $491K) despite similar active student counts across all three centers.

## Recommendations

1. **Build a referral program** — formalize word-of-mouth with incentives. 21.7% close rate makes this the highest-leverage growth lever available.
2. **Prioritize phone leads** — implement same-day callback policy for phone inquiries given 2.5× conversion advantage.
3. **Re-evaluate events** — 517 inquiries yielding 2 enrollments suggests events attract browsers, not buyers. Redirect budget to referral and organic search.
4. **Fix the booking bottleneck** — only 13% of inquiries set an appointment. A/B test follow-up cadence, messaging, and scheduling friction to move this number.
5. **Study JP's enrollment approach** — JP completes 84.5% of recommended program hours vs. AL at 51.9%. Documenting and replicating JP's consultation approach could lift completion rates across all centers.
